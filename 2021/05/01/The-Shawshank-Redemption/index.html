The Shawshank Redemption

Stefan Pitigoi
2021-05-01

The Shawshank Redemption, directed by Frank Darabont, is a peculiar movie filmed almost entirely inside of a prison known as Shawshank. This interesting idea is brought to life by the great acting and plot, which follows the life of Andy Dufresne, but from the eyes of another prisoner named Red. Andy was a former banker accused of murdering his ex-wife and her new lover in cold blood. Although many would assume that life in prison is bleak and depressing, Andy tried his best to make it as enjoyable as possible. He was very unproblematic and did not conflict often with other prisoners. He quickly became friends with another convict, Ellis “Red” Redding, and together they formed a very special bond, which was characterized by their willingness to find redemption through acts of common decency.

Interestingly, it is found towards the end of the show that Andy is in fact innocent, and that his killer was imprisoned with a man that was transferred to Shawshank. Andy had come to accept that he had wronged society and strived to redeem himself even though he was innocent. He worked hard to make the lives of the convicts better in anyway possible. He repeatedly wrote letters to gain access to more books for the prison library and even helped prisoners study to pass their highschool exams. On top of that he put his book keeping skills to work, doing taxes for almost every prison guard and even the prison warden himself. This instills the mood of the film and shows how someone in a very unfortunate position can still find happiness. Andy was satisfied with his life because he was helping so many people, even when society had failed him (falsely imprisoned him and ruined his life). It teaches the viewer how a persons life can be fulfilled when they are selfless and hardworking.

During his time in prison he had trouble with a group of men that wanted him sexually. After refusing, he got beat up multiple times a week for a long time. In terms of camera techniques, Andy’s pain and battle scars from his beatings were not usually shown for long or at all. The director used medium or long camera shots to give Andy his space just like all the other prisoners. Throughout the film, the audience is enamored by the life of Andy, but we are kept in the dark about his feelings, secrets and especially what he does in his cell. Due to the fact that the movie is from the perspective of Red, Andy becomes a mysterious, but very interesting protagonist. The camera reflects this mysteriousness by keeping the angles less personal. Andy is often filmed in shots from more distant locations or is filmed up close only when he is in the scene with someone else. In this way, the audience gets the sense that Andy is more isolated or keeps to himself. The viewer is left wanting to know more about him and eventually these techniques pay off to make the ending more surprising.

Overall, the movie is one of the best I’ve ever seen as it touches on an original idea that lacks the cliches that I hate. The acting is really spectacular and it is one of the greatest stories that I’ve seen in film. I would definietly recommend this movie to someone else because it truely inspires the viewer. If someone with nothing, who is trapped in a prison can do so much, then anyone can.

Rating: 10/10

Works Cited:
Darabont, Frank. The Shawshank Redemption. Columbia Pictures, 1994.
