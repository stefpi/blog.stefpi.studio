Forrest Gump

Stefan Pitigoi
2021-05-24

The multi-award winning film Forrest Gump directed by Robert Zemeckis and starring Tom Hanks is a roller coaster of a movie. It takes a viewer close to the life of a young boy named Forrest, who has an IQ of 75 and several other issues, but nowhere in the movie do you see those problems hinder his quality of life. Forrest is the embodiment of hope for the audience and often he succeeded without even considering what he is actually doing.

For example, while running away from childhood bullies, Forrest runs through a football game, which then gets him scouted to the Alabama State team. In his mind all he was doing was running for the team, something that he really liked to do, and without even noticing he had led his team to winning the championship. Another time, after the Vietnam war and the death of his dear friend Bubba, Forest went to buy a shrimping boat and live out the dream they had talked about. His resolve to keep his promise to Bubba and some luck led him to making a fortune in the shrimping business even though he never intended to. To me these events are so inspiring and comedic because there are so many people in the world working themselves to the bone to get all these achievements and Forrest does them without noticing. This is because Forrest focuses on the small things that make him happy instead of waiting to succeed to feel accomplished. In a world where he was ridiculed for being “stupid” he managed to become so accomplished, which is truly inspiring.

I had previously watched this movie when I was a lot younger and I really did not like it. Being young I did not get a lot of the references and found myself bored with the 3 hour long film. I was more interested in the flashy action packed movies such as Star Wars. I am so glad that I got the chance to re-watch it now because I got to see what it was actually about. There are so many historical events included and it is so funny to watch Forrest’s experiences during those events. One of my favourite scenes is when Forrest meets JFK at the white house and instead of thanking him for inviting him, Forrest says that he has to pee on national television. When he gets access to JFK’s private bathroom, the prop design makes the scene even funnier because right next to the toilet is a red phone, the one used by presidents to open direct communication to Russia. Forrest’s care free attitude is something that many of us viewers need to incorporate in our lives and is one of the reasons why he is such a likable character. Even with so many setbacks he manages to be so happy all the time because he just goes with the flow. His innocence leads him to make the best out of every situation, even when he is involved in the worst events, such as the Vietnam war.

Overall, Forrest Gump is a brilliant movie about the life of one person and everything he managed to do (which was a whole lot). The movie is funny, sad and serious all at the same time and might possibly give you insight into many of the problems you are having. That is why I consider this movie inspirational. Forrest is an icon of hope. His character proves so many different life lessons. “If you put your mind to something you can do it”, “Enjoy the journey, not the destination” and so many more. The film is truly amazing, and unlike my younger self, I really enjoyed watching it. From the acting to the plot to the camera direction the movie is amazing and a must watch.

Rating: 10/10

Works Cited:
Zemeckis, Robert. Forrest Gump. Paramount Pictures, 1994.
