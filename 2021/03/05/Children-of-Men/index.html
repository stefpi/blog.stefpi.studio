Children of Men

Stefan Pitigoi
2021-03-05

The movie Children of Men, directed by Alfonso Cuarón, explores Britain in 2027, during a world crisis. The plot is a depiction of a world where humanity is infertile. What if humanity could not procreate? It follows Theo, who is introduced as an anti-hero, a bystander who is not keen on helping. He is pressured into aiding his ex-wife, Julian, to transport a pregnant lady, Kee, across the country and into the safety of The Human Project. The Human Project is a fabled group of people that were working towards curing the infertility of humanity. As the story progresses, so does Theo and by the end, we see him as a courageous or even heroic individual.

The movie builds a plot in a way that can be differentiated from traditional movies; where the heroic protagonist is very evident. The film makes it obvious who the bad guys are, which is the totalitarian and fascist British government, but hides the protagonist for the first half of the movie. In order to protect their citizens from the outside world, the elected government implements anti-immigration policies and deports illegal residents. Unlike other dystopian movies, such as Blade Runner, there is nothing unrealistic, everything portrayed could happen or has happened already. It is difficult to tell if Theo is the hero or not. He worked for the government as a bureaucrat and even when given the opportunity to help, he was reluctant. Julian, the leader of the Fishes (an anti-government group) even offered to give Theo money in exchange for his help, knowing that he would be stubborn. Later on in the movie, after Julian’s passing, Theo finds out that her death was arranged in order for Luke to rise to power and use Kee as a bargaining chip. At this point Theo’s character changes, he suddenly feels connected to Kee and needs to get her out of there. From there on he becomes the hero the audience expects, his selflessness overtakes him and he realizes his true role. It removes the notion of the classical Hollywood film attributes; the hero did not start as a hero. Also, Theo did not get the girl and we don’t know if everyone lived happily ever after. Alfonso tries to emphasize how anyone can be hero and how they do not have to be flashy, rich or famous. Overall, the plot of the movie develops the characters in a good way that inevitably leads to the film being very entertaining to watch.

Furthermore, the movie hides a deeper meaning and a strong message. It serves to reopen our eyes to what is truly important in our lives. To prove this point, the plot uses the symbol of Kee’s child. In the final scenes, while the military is raiding a building, searching for terrorists, Kee’s baby starts crying. Immediately, the warzone goes silent, people stop screaming and guns stop firing, which is an example of real sound being used to create a surreal effect. The child represents everything good in the world. It represents peace, innocence, and hope. The movie has shown the viewer a world without children, which is full of hatred, violence, and depression. It is trying to remind us of what we have forgotten, that life without the next generation is not a life worth living. This generation is trading quick satisfaction for sustainability and within the next few years we might begin to feel the side effects. Pollution and our general disregard for the planet are changing life (climate change, pandemics, etc.), but the film emphasizes a path to redemption through the character arc of Theo. His ultimate sacrifice for humanity shows the second part of the message; we can change. Theo, who is depicted as an average citizen, is capable of making change, just like any of us are.

Ultimately, Children of Men, directed by Alfonso Caurón, is an enjoyable film to watch and an effective example of the dystopian genre. The movie incorporates a well developed plot, that exposes the dystopian society and also creates interesting situations that develop the characters. Additionally, the flick exposes a strong message about our lifestyles, which gets the viewer to reflect on the impact of their own actions on the future of mankind. I would rate this movie an 7/10 because of the depth of the message the director is trying to send. Dystopian movies are not really my favourite as the films can come across as corny because of how unrealistic they are. The movie did have some flaws in acting, which aren’t necessarily correlated with the director’s vision, therefore keeping the score higher. I enjoyed pondering the theme greatly after the film and also appreciated how down-to-earth the movie was, and how most of the events reflect real world crises. Seeing as I enjoyed it, I would recommend for all of you to watch it because I believe you would too.

Rating: 7/10

Works Cited:
Cuarón, Alfonso. Children of Men. Strike Entertainment, Hit and Run Productions, 2006.
