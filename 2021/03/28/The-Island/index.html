The Island

Stefan Pitigoi
2021-03-28

I never thought of Michael Bay as a great director because all the movies that I have seen him produced are straightforward, cookie-cutter films with too much money spent on destroying things. Unfortunately, the movie The Island does not change that notion for me.

The film explores an interesting dystopian world, where people will be able to clone themselves, but the idea is actually a copy of the 1979 sci-fi movie The Clonus Horror. Both plots follow the narrative of an isolated colony living under the rule of mysterious overlords and whose lives are controlled by a myth of a remote paradise. In both films, the main characters leave the colony and find the truth about the purpose of the clones. Even without the plagiarism, the plot in the movie is not exactly good.

There are several critical points that diminish the merit of the film, the such as its over the top action violence, trashy Hollywood narratives and cliché scenes. There are many weird details that the movie explores, which are mostly unnecessary, such as the sex life of a side character played by Steve Buscemi. I often find Hollywood movies exploring topics that are not necessary at all to the storyline, but rather attract viewers because of sex appeal. I believe that the beginning half of the movie was restrained, but in the second half there was an explosion of classic, very cliché scenes that were not appealing and did not add anything to the movie. Even Michael Bay’s infamous action scenes are not that entertaining. To some degree, yes, they may have been exhilarating or suspenseful, but they were not anything special. Things blow up, fall from the sky and miraculously, both of the main characters are unharmed, crazy, right?

What was partially interesting about the film was the double feature aspect. The movie was divided into two parts, shifting from spooky sci-fi to high-tech action. I have never seen a film with such a big switch halfway through, so it was definitely a thought-provoking experience.

Ultimately, the overall idea of the conflict between human dignity and human cloning is a very interesting and entertaining topic to explore, but is greatly overshadowed by the movies flaws. I would rate this movie 4/10 and honestly its not worth your time to watch it. The idea is cool, but I think imagining what would happen would be more entertaining than actually watching The Island.

Rating: 4/10

Works Cited:
Bay, Michael. The Island. DreamWorks Distribution, 2005.
