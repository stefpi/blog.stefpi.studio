Parasite

Stefan Pitigoi
2021-06-05

Parasite (noun) is an organism that lives in or on another organism and benefits by deriving nutrients at the other’s expense. The movie Parasite (2019) is a film about class struggle and its character’s resemblances to parasites. The Kim family was a very poor family that lived in a slum in South Korea and because of the insanely competitive job market they made money folding pizza boxes. When one of Ki-woo’s old friends gave him the opportunity to work as a tutor he took it and began to tutor the Park family’s daughter. Eventually the Kim family devised a plan to all get hired for the Park family as unrelated, highly qualified staff. Everything was fine until they discovered a secret under the house, a basement where the old housekeeper’s husband had been living for many years.

The movie shows parasitic relationships between the Kim and Park family because both families leech off of each other’s resources. The Kims use the Parks for their money and the Parks use the Kims to do all the jobs that they don’t want to do. This toxic relationship shows the attitudes that the different social classes have towards each other. A key part of the movie is Mr.Park’s disgust towards Mr.Kim’s smell saying that he smells sour, like a basement. This signifies Mr.Park’s attitude that he is better that Mr.Kim, and that he has provided better for his family than Mr.Kim had. This relationship is very well shown in the scenes where Mr.Kim is driving Mr.Park around as his chauffer. Mr.Park is captured in more a comfortable position, leaning back and relaxed, signifying his dominance in the relationship. Mr.Kim is captured in medium close up shots that show him in a more tense position with both hands on the sterring wheel. He also seems agitated because of how he looks back to talk to Mr.Park (because eye contact is more respectful). Mr.Kim is always shown trying to impress Mr.Park no matter how disrespectful he is in return (until the end, of course). Scenes such as this one are so amazingly captured and bring so much to the story with such subtle techniques.

Costume design is also a very big part of this movie as the Kim family is seen wearing dirty old clothes, while the Parks are always wearing expensive, clean garments. Even the physical locations of their houses show the classism in the city. The Parks live on top of a hill, and they say that they love to go out in the rain. On the other side of the city, rain poses a very big problem for the Kims, and floods their house because they live at the bottom of a hill. Every single aspect of the film shows the difference in attitude between the rich and the poor, which is why this movie is made so well. There is so much to unpack and so many levels to each scene which is a truly incredible feat.

The second half was more of a thriller, but it served to show how people of lower social classes end up fighting in-between each other instead of banding together to succeed. In the final scene, where Geun-sae comes out of the basement on a murder spree, it is clear to see the divide between the two social classes. While the poor fight for their lives and try to keep each other alive, the rich run away, and Mr.Park goes as far to ask for his keys while Mr.Kim’s daughter is dying right in front of him. The film ends in a very gruesome way, with the Kims losing their daughter and with Mr.Kim taking Geun-sae’s place in the basement. It is a horrifying ending because of the fact that the Parks ended up relatively ok, the only ones that lost where the poor.

Ultimately, the movie Parasite is a crazy, but realistic rendition of the social classes and their relationships. I really enjoyed this movie and the depths of its theme that it displayed, but I was expecting a little more comedy. I can really vouch for serious, psychological thrillers, but for a movie to really make the top of my charts it has to be able to mix in the comedy. I think that it may have been quite impossible to do so because of how serious the theme was meant to be, but it was very heavy, that’s for sure. Overall, I think it was a great movie and I would definitely recommend it to everyone. If you say you don’t want to watch it because you have to read subtitles, you should reconsider because you are missing out on one of the most highly regarded films of the decade.

Rating: 9/10

Works Cited:
Ho, Bong Joon. Parasite. Neon, 2019.
